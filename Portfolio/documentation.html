How I made my website

The main structure that you can find on most of my pages is that I divided every page into 3 main structures, nav, main, & footer. Other than that within those pages I contained most elements within divs, and if I needed to contain elements that rested on the same vertical axis I contained them within a flexbox and spaced them out evenly between each other with a justify-content:space-between command.

To make the drop down javascript menu on the mobile view of my pages, i used javascript to generate the width of the menu so that it could animate on-screen upon being clicked by the user. On-top of that I made sure to vigorously comment my code throughout the process, in order to make sure I understood what I was doing and to make my code more organized.

Challenges

One of the main challenges of making this site was converting my design to code. Starting the project I sketched out my design and mocked up the final output in Adobe Illustrator. I wanted to go for a minimal but sleek, and futuristic style. However converting this designs to code wasn’t going to be as simple as I thought it would be.

I feel that my homepage was the most indicative of this problem, as its design wasn’t the most friendly to me at first. It wasn’t the most traditional way to structure one’s page and as such it required a lot of tweaking. I had to scrap the my original logo design, and it took the longest time to make sure the opening card floated above the decorative black lines at the top of the home screen. In addition it took a lot of fiddling around to make sure my project elements stuck to the edge of the screen. Ultimately when I finished designing the homepage I realized that this format wouldn’t work for a mobile view, so I had to end up creating little preview cards for the projects I wanted to show on my portfolio which was another challenge that I had to face.

The most enjoyable parts to work on

The resume, about pages were fun to create, in addition to the footer element at the bottom of every page, as I feel that they all do a good job of adding levity and charm to my page, in addition helping the viewer get to know me.
