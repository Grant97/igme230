What's Your Odds?

Odds of Survival

Odds of survival is a wildife education app. designed to provide animal safety tips under the framing of how likely you are to survival an encounter with them.

ROLE

Concept, Visual Design & Interaction Design

WHEN

Spring 2017

CLIENT

School

01

Problem

People aren’t engaged in nature, because engaing with nature has become optional. As such, many people lack important survvial skills

02

Research

How do informational applications provide convinient access to information while still being visually engaing?

When I began the creative process for Odds of Survival, I wanted to figure out how to create an application with a distinct visual style that is both informative and visually engaging. I mainly aimed my research around phone applications and interactive websites, as I find that they tend to have much more visually striking designs as they have to use them to engage new users in a very short amount of time.

Main Takeaways

Large scale Database

Many wildlife applications feature large-scale databases of animal that the curious user can select and learn about, in a quick and convinient way.

Interactive Article

Interactive pages highlighting a specific animal, with overlayed video footage of the animal in their habitat, or some representation of that animal.

Engaging Visualization

Parts of an application that may have otherwise been mundane are given interactive and interesting visualization via particle effects, three dimensionality, etc, in order to capture user attention.

Effective Design

Strong design principles and and well thought out layout, which leads to these reoccuring traits.

  1. Panel based information Display

  2. Strong type hierarchy

  3. Proximity based data display

04

Iterations

#1. Scenario Creation

My first iteration aimed to design Odds of Survival as mainly a scenario creation application, where users can create scenarios based on their location, what activity they are doing and when they are doing it. In return Odds of Survival would provide users with specialized animal information based on the animals you were the most likely to see in your area, and your Odds of Survival if you were to encounter them, along with additional survival info.

#2. Geographically Centered Info.

The second iteration was very similar to my final composition. In this iteration the user would be launched right into the view of a U.S. map where they could tap a state and find different animal related incidents. Accompanying this paradigm was a database system, where users could look up specific animals in alphabetical order or under different categories, such as most dangerous, or most aggressive.

05

Solution

Upon critique from my peers, I found that I was going in the right direction, but I needed to expand upon this direction, and give it a more striking visual style.

FEATURE HIGHLIGHTS

Learn about the animals in your area.

Sweep through animal related incidents near you with the map view. Just select your desired state, and you'll be able to learn how often animal related incidents happen during any season.

Animal safety info right at your finger tips.

Upon selecting an animal you'll be able to learn about preventative measures to ensure your safety, directions in case of an encounter and general info about the animal.

Gain intimate knowledge of your selected animal.

Get to know what your selected animals look like with 3-D recreations of them on their general info page. In addition learn about iconic traits of the animal with the highlight system, which provides interactive reticles on key parts of the 3-D animal's body.
